On a remarkably unremarkable change

April 3, 2021

This is weirdly written, but written nonetheless.

Dusting off my camera has been a peculiar experience. Part of it is has been like riding a bike - all the dials and clicks still feel about right. A little awkward, but right. Part of it feels completely different, as if I am engaging in a different craft than that of a past life, one where I would greedily capture moments without a second care. Learning to film my current realities has made me painfully cognizant of the cycle my life (and perhaps most of the world) has drifted into. A monotonous interchanging between moments of work and wandering, those of painful stillness and ignorant action.

There is no sole perpetrator that has sent me adrift. Sure, a global pandemic, heartbreak and dashes of existential dread could be blamed, but they all ultimately fall under the umbrella that is living this very reality.

And so, I mourn the many causes of this frighteningly normal, reactionary pattern of living. I mourn the past selves that I can no longer bring out at will, the realities I once knew that now feel as fleeting as dreams. Perhaps I even mourn for my present reality, for it too will not last forever.

Melancholic as this all sounds, this blunt introspection also yields fascination. There is nothing special about all these rambly thoughts, people change all the time, everywhere. It is quite simple - unremarkable, if you will. But that very fundamental phenomena is a small part of the grand scheme of things. And the minute intricacies of the latter is what keeps this life interesting, does it not?

Perhaps I have learned that change is normal. That change, as intrusive of a force it is on our present selves, is also gently knit into the fabric of living. Perhaps I will change to one day be able to embrace change without mourning the past, without anxiously peaking at the future. Or perhaps I have learned nothing at all, and this is all just nonsense embeded in the chaos that is this brain of mine.

(un)Remarkable.
