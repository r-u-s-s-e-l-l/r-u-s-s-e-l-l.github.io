playing tourist (day 1)

Our first full day in Japan started off my heading back to Shinjuku station so we could take a train to the Harajuku neighbourhood. We got lost right from the get-go as we couldn’t find the office to exchange our JR Pass vouchers ahaha :(

After exploring (aka walking in an unnecessary circle), we found ourselves at the popular Meiji Shrine. While the heat was, well, heat, the walk was still very serene thanks to the shade and the forestry.

We changed pace by wandering the funky streets of Harajuku. Even though I was planning on only shopping at the end of our trip, I couldn’t resist thrifting a bit of beige clothing.

Heading towards the ever-so popular Shibuya crossing, we picked up some delicious eats and got our first conveyor belt sushi experience. We continued playing tourist by seeing Tokyo from the government housed observatory.

Although it doesn’t seem like much, it was a long day with a lot to take in. A good twelve hours.
